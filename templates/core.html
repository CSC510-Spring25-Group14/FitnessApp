Core Conditioning

Develop a strong core for more than the six-pack abs that will hopefully peak through. Use core conditioning to improve your overall athletic performance and lifeâ€”the flat abs are just a bonus.

Main Practice:

  • Plank: Maintaining a straight, rigid body position, supported by your forearms and toes, challenges the entire core. It is a fundamental core exercise that targets not only the abdominal muscles but also the back and shoulders.
  • Reverse Crunch: Lie on your back with your hands by your sides or under your lower back for support. Bring your knees towards your chest until your hips are slightly off the ground and perform the crunch.
  • Bird Dog Crunch: Start on all fours in a tabletop position. Extend one arm forward and the opposite leg back, forming a straight line, and perform the crunch.
  • Glute Bridge: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Engage your core and glutes to lift your hips, forming a straight line from your knees to shoulders.
  • Russian Twist: Sit on the floor, lean back slightly, and twist your torso from side to side, optionally holding a weight for added resistance.
